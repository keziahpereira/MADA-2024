Tasks 1 and 2

What do you want your audience to take away from the website?

  • Empower students to make meals with whatever ingredients they have, reducing food waste.
  • Understand how to plan meals without buying extra ingredients.
  • Experience a sense of community around sustainability and food scarcity.
  • Build confidence in cooking by providing simple, accessible recipes.
  • Become aware of the impact of food waste and the importance of using ingredients wisely.
  • Improve budgeting skills by minimizing unnecessary food purchases.

How will my website help my audience?

The Student Pantry will help users in several key ways:

  • Provide recipe suggestions based on available ingredients.
  • Include difficulty levels to cater to all cooking skills.
  • Offer a gamified experience to keep users engaged during cooking.
  • Feature navigable tools for easy ingredient search and future multi-language support.
  • Encourage sustainability by promoting better use of ingredients and reducing waste.

Narrative and Structure

The primary objective of "The Student Pantry" is to empower students to make the most of the ingredients they already have, promoting sustainability and minimizing food waste. The website aims to help students create meals with available resources and educate them on the importance of using ingredients efficiently. Specific outcomes include:

  • Skills: Users will learn to create meals from available ingredients, improving their cooking skills without needing extra purchases.
  • Experience: Students will interact with the platform by searching for recipes based on what they have on hand and engage in fun, interactive games while cooking.
  • Ability: The website will increase students' ability to cook with fewer resources and reduce food waste.
  • New Perspectives: The website will encourage students to think more sustainably about food and waste.

Audience Understanding

The target audience is primarily university students, especially at the University of Glasgow, with plans to expand to other universities. They likely have:

  • Basic Cooking Knowledge: Most students are familiar with basic cooking techniques but may struggle with making meals from limited ingredients or within time constraints.
  • Interests: They may be interested in saving time, cooking efficiently, and reducing food waste. Sustainability may also appeal to them as it is a growing concern.
  • Lifestyle Needs: University students are often busy, live in shared housing, and are on tight budgets, making a focus on easy, cost-effective meals important.

Structuring The Narrative

  • Thematic: Organize the website around key themes like "Quick Meals," "Sustainable Cooking," and "Ingredients on Hand." This allows users to easily find relevant recipes or tips based on their immediate needs or concerns.
  • Methodological: Provide a clear, step-by-step process for each recipe, with easy instructions and difficulty levels to cater to users with different cooking experiences.
  • Interactive/Engaging: A waiting game while meals cook could be both a fun and practical tool. Features like "Ingredient Challenges" or "Leftovers Transformation" would engage students creatively.
  • Chronological: The website could feature a "meal prep journey" section, guiding users from ingredient selection to cooking, with each step accompanied by helpful tips or videos.

Audience Engagement

Given the student demographic, the content will use an informal tone, addressing users directly in the second person ("you"). Simple, friendly language will make the content feel approachable and encourage engagement.

By considering these factors, "The Student Pantry" will be a structured, engaging platform that helps students enhance their cooking skills, save time, and embrace sustainable food habits while making the most out of what they have.

Plan to Build the Website

  1. Will define core functions:
    • Ingredient-based recipe search.
    • Recipe difficulty levels.
    • Gamified cooking timer/game.
    • Dietary filtering (Vegan, Vegetarian, Non-Veg).
  2. Design the user flow:
    • Map out the user interaction process, ensuring a smooth journey from ingredient input to recipe suggestion.
    • Design key pages: Homepage, Ingredient Search, Recipe Results, Cooking Timer/Game, and About/Community Info.
  3. Create wireframes and layout:
    • Design a clean, student-friendly interface.
    • Align with the moodboard for a cohesive visual aesthetic.
  4. Develop the backend:
    • Set up the recipe database and matching algorithms.
    • Implement dietary filters and logic for the recipe suggestions.
  5. Develop the frontend:
    • Code the interface with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for user interaction.
    • Ensure accessibility and responsive design for all devices.
  6. Test and refine:
    • Test with a small audience and refine based on feedback.
  7. Launch and collect feedback:
    • Launch and monitor user engagement, gather ongoing feedback for improvements.

Reflection

The Student Pantry hopes to address real-world challenges of university students by offering practical meal solutions, reducing food waste, and supporting sustainability efforts. This project aims to empower students with cooking skills and build a sense of community. It reflects my belief that food should not be wasted and that students deserve a platform to create nourishing, affordable meals with whatever they have available.
