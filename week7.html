Task 2: Analysis of Food Generators

All three generators (Supercook, DishGen, and Tesco) utilize high-quality, appealing food imagery to engage users. These images help increase user engagement and enhance information retention. Visually attractive food photos are strategically used to show the results of recipes, which helps users visualize the end product.

  1. 1. Alignment with Website Purpose

    • Supercook: Focuses on practical meal creation by leveraging user-inputted ingredients. Images support the idea of making cooking with limited ingredients more achievable.
    • DishGen: Aligns its imagery with the AI-powered recipe generator concept, appealing to businesses by showcasing AI-generated food images that could be used for product promotions or marketing.
    • Tesco: Uses food images to inspire users and highlight meal-planning features while subtly promoting products available in its stores, aligning with its goal of making meal planning more convenient and driving in-store purchases.

  2. 2. Types of Images

    All three websites use raster images of food, as they provide realistic and appetizing visuals. The choice of raster images is deliberate, as they show the actual outcome of recipes and serve to guide users visually.

  3. 3. Intentional Image Use for Marketing and Functionality

    Images on these platforms aren't just aesthetic—they serve a functional purpose. They enhance information comprehension by showing users exactly what they can expect from the recipe. This is important for retention, as users are more likely to engage with content that visually represents their potential outcome. For example, Tesco’s use of food photography is closely tied to their product offerings.

  4. 4. Practical and User-Friendly Design

    Each platform employs images thoughtfully to enhance the user experience:

    • Supercook: Uses images to help users understand what meals they can make with their available ingredients.
    • DishGen: Showcases the potential of AI-generated recipes for marketing.
    • Tesco: While the food photography is effective, the overall layout could be improved for a better user experience.

Key takeaway: The use of high-quality food imagery is a consistent strategy across all three websites, aimed at increasing user engagement and supporting the websites' goals.

Editing Digital Images

Analysis

In my opinion, optimising images brings many benefits, such as faster loading times, reduced bandwidth usage, and a better overall experience for users. However, these advantages often come at the cost of visual quality, particularly in terms of colour and resolution. I think when it comes to image formats, RGB is by far the superior option for high-quality content, especially when engaging visuals are the goal. Greyscale images are useful when the intention is to emphasise simplicity or artistic elements, while indexed images, though more efficient, fall short in terms of visual appeal. For my project, I believe using RGB images at 800px with 72ppi and 1000px with 90ppi strikes the best balance between image quality and web page loading speed.
